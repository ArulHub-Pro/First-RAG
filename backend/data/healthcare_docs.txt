Diabetes is a chronic disease where blood sugar levels are too high. 
Common symptoms include frequent urination, excessive thirst, fatigue, blurred vision, and slow healing wounds.

Malaria is a mosquito-borne disease caused by Plasmodium parasites.
Symptoms include fever, chills, sweating, headache, nausea, and fatigue.

Hypertension is a condition where blood pressure is consistently too high.
It increases the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Dengue is a viral infection spread by mosquitoes.
Symptoms include high fever, severe headache, joint pain, muscle pain, and rash.

Common cold is a viral infection affecting the nose and throat.
Symptoms include cough, sore throat, sneezing, and mild fever.
